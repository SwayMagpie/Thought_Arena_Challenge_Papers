## 1. Introduction: Why Do You Seek “Reassurance” So Excessively?
Why do you seek reassurance so intensely?
Why do you react so strongly to even small uncertainties?
Why are you told you’re “too cautious” in relationships and social interactions?

The reason is not your personality, your weakness, or your past experiences.

It is because your brain is still optimized for an ancient environment.

In order for humans to survive in the past,
having a strong sense of vigilance was essential.

- Detecting danger

- Anticipating worst‑case scenarios

- Not trusting others easily

- Being highly sensitive to uncertainty

These traits were directly tied to survival.

Individuals with stronger vigilance
gathered more information, predicted more accurately,
and survived more successfully.

Vigilance evolved **together with intelligence** as a survival strategy.

So it is completely natural that you “seek reassurance too much.”
Your brain is optimized for an environment full of danger.

But—

**The modern world is no longer that environment.**

Physical threats have drastically decreased.
In relationships, work, and social life,
being “too vigilant” has become a disadvantage.

Yet your brain continues to operate
according to the rules of the old environment.

This produces:

- Excessive negative vigilance, and

- An abnormally high cost required to feel safe.

The discomfort you feel in life is not your fault.
Your brain is simply overadapted to a world that no longer exists.

Your vigilance evolved to protect you—
but now, it works against you.
